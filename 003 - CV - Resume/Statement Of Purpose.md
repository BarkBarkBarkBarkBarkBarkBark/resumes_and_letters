**[Statement of Purpose]{.underline}**

Untreated mental illness and addiction are the norm at the homeless shelter where psychological awareness is critical to avoid re-traumatization. Using data from a county-wide database of homeless persons, I've identified factors that correlate with outcomes in order to emphasize those services. There is a need for increased awareness of mental health disorders; especially schizophrenia, PTSD, and addiction.

My academic interest is to understand the processes giving rise to thought and mental imagery. As an undergraduate at UCD, I asked Dr. Lauren Leitz, "What is a thought?" She replied, "An active set of neurons," which haunts me. I suspect a thought is predictive mental imagery mediated by the cortico-basal ganglia-thalamic loop, layer specific activity of cortical columns, and the modulatory effects of monoamines. A clinically relevant approach to my interest is the pathogenesis and treatment of hallucinations, delusions, and affective dysregulation.

While studying Neuroscience at UCD, courses such as neural mechanisms of behavior and comparative neuroanatomy gave me a foundational understanding of neural processes. At Arizona State University I learned traditional psychological frameworks in classes such as cognitive science and psychopathology. I enjoy a multi-leveled appreciation of behavior, from cell processes to social factors. The academic rigor, social atmosphere, and deep community roots via the hospital are my motivations for applying with UCD.

The research of the Mangun, and Hasting, Luck, and Carter lab match my interests. The Hasting Lab's investigation of adaptive emotional behaviors aligns with my goal of understanding maladaptive trauma responses.
